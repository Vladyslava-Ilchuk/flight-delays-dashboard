## 📌 Overview

This project presents an interactive **Tableau dashboard** designed to analyze and visualize flight delays and cancellations across the United States.  

It simulates a real-world data analytics scenario for a **transport authority or aviation analytics department**, focusing on operational efficiency, airline performance, and airport congestion patterns.

The goal is to transform raw aviation data into actionable insights through interactive visual analytics.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

The dashboard is designed to answer key operational and strategic questions such as:

- What are the main causes of flight delays and cancellations?
- Which airlines show the highest and lowest performance reliability?
- How do airports differ in terms of congestion and efficiency?
- What time-based patterns can be observed in flight disruptions?
- How do external factors influence overall flight punctuality?

This analysis can support:
- Aviation authorities  
- Airline operational management  
- Transport planning institutions  
- Data-driven decision-making teams  

---

## 🏗️ Data Model

The dashboard is based on a relational data structure consisting of three core datasets:

- **Flights Table** → flight-level operational metrics  
- **Airlines Table** → airline identifiers and metadata  
- **Airports Table** → airport location and classification data

These datasets are joined to enable multi-dimensional analysis across time, geography, and airline performance.

---

## 📊 Dashboard Features

The Tableau dashboard includes the following interactive components:

- 📈 KPI overview (total flights, delay rate, cancellation rate)
- ⏱️ Time-series analysis of delays and cancellations
- ✈️ Airline performance comparison
- 🏢 Airport-level efficiency analysis
- ❌ Breakdown of cancellation causes
- 🗺️ Geographic visualization of flight activity
- 🎛️ Interactive filters
- 🔄 Parameters and dashboard actions for dynamic exploration

## 🔍 Key Insights

The dashboard enables identification of several important patterns:

- Certain airlines consistently experience higher delay rates under specific operational conditions  
- Major hub airports show significantly higher congestion-related delays  
- Weather-related disruptions are a dominant cause of cancellations  
- Delay patterns vary across time of day and seasonality  
- Regional airports demonstrate higher variability in operational performance
